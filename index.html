About Me

I have always had a passion for building things. This can be seen from my hobbies and previous work experience. I enjoy taking a project from concept to finished product.

My background was in rebuilding large-frame generators for multinational power generation companies. Prior to that I was a machinist stationed aboard the USS Emory S. Land in the Navy.

Before learning web development, I was majoring in mechanical engineering at the University of Wisconsin- Milwaukee. While attending UWM I owned and operated an automotive repair business with a focus on european cars.

At Emory University, I helped the engineers and doctors in the Department of Biomedical Informatics. I was tasked with the development of several web applications used in their research. The interfaces developed are designed to annotate over different waveforms to check patient heart activity and sound. I worked across the entire stack using modern web tools, frameworks, and cloud computing.
